# Lecture 1: Introduction to MLOps

### 🔍 What is this?
**MLOps** stands for **Machine Learning Operations**. In simple terms, it is the practice of taking a machine learning model out of a data scientist's experimental notebook and putting it into the real world where users can benefit from it, safely and reliably. 

Data science is about building a great model once. MLOps is about making sure that model runs, updates, and stays accurate thousands of times a day without breaking.

### 💡 Real-World Analogy
Think of it like running a restaurant. A data scientist is like an elite chef who invents a brilliant new recipe (the model). **MLOps** is the rest of the restaurant infrastructure: the supply chain getting fresh ingredients, the kitchen assembly line, the dishwasher, and the health inspector. Without MLOps, you just have a recipe on a piece of paper, but no way to serve 1,000 customers a night.

### 🔑 Key Pillars
* **Reproducibility:** If your colleague trains a model today, you should be able to get the exact same results tomorrow using the same code and data.
* **Automation:** Replacing manual steps (like manually copying file paths) with automated scripts.
* **Monitoring:** Keeping an eye on live models to ensure they don't start making strange predictions over time.
